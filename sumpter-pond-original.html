The Dredge Pond (Original)

If you journey along the outskirts of Baker City, you may climb up the pass to a small community called Sumpter Valley, basked in gold mining history. Early in the 1920's to the 1950's, three massive gold dredges, the size of hotels, floated along its own dug out ponds, scooping massive amounts of dirt, rocks and material from the ground to sluice it, or separate the gold inside the dirt with water and a washboard style plank. The tailings, or material washed away from the gold, was deposited on a giant conveyer belt, which swayed back and forth to create 9-10 foot tailings piles. These tailings piles, larger than ski moguls, covers so much land in the Sumpter Valley, that you can see it from Google Earthâ„¢! The three dredges collectively recovered four million dollars worth of gold. This piece depicts one of the dredge ponds created by the massive machines, south of the Sumpter Valley Dredge #1, and adjacent to the South Sumpter Valley Dredge trail loop.
