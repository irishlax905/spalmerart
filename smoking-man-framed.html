Smoking Man (Framed)

Created on a black coated clay scratchboard along with ink and quill, this piece is a medium rarely used by most artist. Frankly with its sensory displeasures it creates, it's almost like scratching nails down a chalkboard to scratch away the clay coated surface to reveal the white layer beneath. The subject matter pays homage to texture and texturizing subject matter, like adding extra wrinkles to an old man's portrait. Traditional ink and quill was used on the piece to add additional shadows to highlighted areas, or acting as an 'eraser' for the artist. Once the dark colors are scratched away, adding ink is a great way to restore shading. Scratchboards are also great fun for budding artists. You can find a scratchboard of your own to doodle on at your local art or craft store. It's especially fun to select a scratchboard with a rainbow-colored background so the artwork looks psychedelic at the end!

Framed prints come with glass and are shipped for free anywhere in the US. Poster prints are shipped in mailing tubes. All sales are final.


Where Should I Hang It?

Hang Smoking Man in a hallway, study or dining room where you see dormancy and low activity. This is a piece that brings a lot of neutrals to a room so be sure to have some neutrals in the room to go along with this unique artwork.

Asian Inspired Art

Bordering the piece, states a charm in Japanese handwriting that grants life longevity, good fortune and health to anyone who owns the artwork. If you are putting together a tea room, adding to a calm study or need something to fill up your hallways, this is the item for you.
